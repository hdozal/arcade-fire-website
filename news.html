Arcade Fire will perform at London's Hyde Park on 3 July as part of the British Summer Time festival.

The Grammy award-winning band, who will headline Glastonbury on the Friday night of that festival, are the latest act added to the 2014 British Summer Time lineup. Supporting Arcade Fire will be Jake Bugg, Wild Beasts, Future Islands and Band of Skulls.

They are the prime example of an art rock band who have conquered the mainstream without forsaking the qualities that made them so thrilling in the first place.

And Arcade Fire have been recently energised by the addition of two percussion players and a tour of Latin America.

The Grammy and Brit-awarded multi-million sellers formed shortly after Texas-raised Win Butler met future wife Régine Chassagne at university in Montreal in 2001.

Their collective creativity and loyal fanbase – including U2 and Bruce Springsteen – helped turn them into global stars.
